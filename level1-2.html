Step by step instructions

  1. Stand on your toes at the side of the pole

  2. Take a high grip on the pole with your inside hand (hand closest to the pole)

  3. Stand on your toes with your inside leg and reach out your outer leg and outer hand (arms and legs will form an X)

  4. From here the outer foot will draw a circle across the floor all the way around the pole until you get back to your startposition

  5. The free arm will be extended outwards during the spin to create a sweeping feeling. But the free arm could also be placed on the pole in chest-height to give a bit more stability

  6. When the outer foot lands on the ground you follow with the inside foot that was on the floor so you stand in your startingpostition again

Step by step instructions

  1. Stand on your toes at the side of the pole with your hands in basic grip

  2. Take one step with your inner leg and start swinging your outer leg in a circle and place your heel on the opposite side of the pole. The knee of the leg that is now on the pole should be pushing down towards the floor so that the lower leg is as horisontal as possible

  3. Use the inner leg to push away from the floor, then place the front of the ankle against the pole (opposite of the other legs heel). This legs knee should also be pushing towards the floor so that the knees will create a diamond-shape

  4. Make sure that the lower hand keeps under chest-height to be able to push your body away from the pole. The more distance you get from the pole the easier it will be too get momentum in the spin

  5. Land on your toes when the spin begins to slow down

Step by step instructions

  1. Stand on your toes at the side of the pole, place your hands in basic grip (inner hand high up on the pole and outer hand in waist height)

  2. Lift and extend the inner leg to hook the back of the knee on the pole in thigh height. Make sure to get a good grip in the knee by pushing the hips forward

  3. Get speed with the outer leg by pushing the hips forward and start spinning around the pole. Bend the outer leg to match the hooked inner leg. The legs should be kept in a diamond shape throughout the spin

  4. Press the lower hand against the pole to help push the body outwards throughout the spin

  5. Land either by spinning down until the knees land on the floor or use your hands to lift your body upwards and put your feet on the ground

Step by step instructions

  1. Stand on your toes at the side of the pole, place your hands in basic grip (inner hand high up on the pole and outer hand in waist height)

  2. Lift your inner foot from the floor and bend the leg back, keeping it high up from the floor

  3. The lower hand will have a grip around the pole in chest-height but you can also lay your lower arm against the pole to get more support and pressing the the body away from the pole

  4. Lean the body backwards and out to fall into a big circle around the pole with your back first, try to push your body away from the pole with the lower hand

  5. When the back of the knee of the leg that's left on the ground touches the pole you will lift that leg from the ground and hook it on the pole

  6. Keep your hips pushed forwards during the spin. Keep your knees far apart, while your feet are pointing towards each other in a sort of diamond-position

  7. You can land either by spinning down until your knees land on the floor or by lifting the knee of the pole and landing on your feet with the support of your arms

Step by step instructions

  1. Stand on your toes behind the pole

  2. Grab the pole as high as you can with one of your hands

  3. Put the other arms lower arm against the pole (the hand should be gripping in facial height)

  4. Make sure that the lower arm is straight behind the pole (and not beside it) so that it can't glide to the side even when you put weight against it

  5. Lift one of the legs and put the front of the shin against the pole. In this position the foot should be on one side of the pole and the knee on the other side

  6. Pull down the shoulder of the higher arm so that the shoulders are straight while pushing against the pole with the lower arm. With the help of the stability of the arms and the pressure of the leg against the pole you should lift the other leg of the floor put it around the pole opposite of the first leg. The heel should go around so that your feet are on either way of the pole

  7. Push your legs against the pole and pull with your arms to pull up your body

  8. Come back down again by losening the legs and in a controlled manner lowerering the feet down to the floor

Step by step instructions

  1. Stand on your toes in front of the pole

  2. Lean the upper body a little bit to the side and backwards so that the waist gets a grip on the pole

  3. Place the inner arm around the pole all the way into the armpit, the elbow should be pointing downwards, and the hand should grip the pole in shoulder height. Place the other hand right above the inner hand. Make sure the hands are not placed higher than the head

  4. The legs should swing in a big circular movement. The inner leg should swing outwards past the outer leg, up and then back and land it on the other side of the pole, follow with the other leg (think of the movement a bit like a windmill). Think about keeping the legs far from each other to get a big split in the movement (depending on flexibility)

  2. Place your hands in basic grip (high grip on the pole with the inner hand, outer hand grips the pole in chest height)

  3. Take a step with the outer foot and lift the inner foot from the floor with the leg bent backwards, high up from the ground

  4. Twist and lean your body backwards to fall in a big circle around the pole with your back first, put the lower hands lower arm against the pole to help support you in pushing your body away from the pole

  5. Instead of hooking your inner leg like in Backhook, put the outer (now inner leg) legs thigh against the pole and lift the legs so that they are in an S shaped position (the angle in both legs should be about 90 degrees) and keep the legs as horizontal as you can

  6. Land by either spinning down to the floor or by with the support of your arms lowering your feet to the floor

Step by step instructions

  1. Stand on your toes in front of the pole

  2. Lean your body backwards so that your shoulders and chest are behind the pole while the butt and legs are still in front of the pole. Make sure you get a good grip with the waist on the pole

  3. Move the inner arm around the pole, put lower arm against the pole and grab it in shoulder height, elbow pointing down. The other hand should be placed right above the inner hand. Make sure the hands are not placed above the head

  4. Activate your core to lift and bend the legs, they should go on either side of the body so that knees are pointing towards the shoulders

  5. When you are lifting your legs, you should also be leaning back so that your back are as horizontal with the floor as possible

  6. When done, slowly lower your body back to the floor

Step by step instructions

  1. Stand on your toes at the side of the pole

  2. Put your hands in basic grip (high grip on the pole with the inner hand, outer hand grips the pole in chest height)

  3. Take a step with the inner leg and then start sweeping with the outer leg around the pole to get speed

  4. While your sweeping the outer leg lift the inner leg up and then collect the legs so that they are next to each other in a 90 degrees angle (like you are sitting on an invisible chair)

  5. Activate the core and use the lower hand to push the body away from the pole. You should be spinning around the pole with the side of your body towards the pole

  6. Focus on lowering the shoulders and activating the back throughout the spin

  7. Land on your toes when the spin begins to slow down
